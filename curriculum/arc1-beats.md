# Arc 1 «La antena» (días 1–30) + Arc 2 opening (31–35) — Beat sheets & vocab schedule

Format per day: story beats · Mundo Real topic · new vocabulary (9–11 items, the ONLY
new items scripts may introduce that day). Recycling is computed from offsets
[1, 3, 7, 21] days back. Stage S1 (days 1–30): see level-map.json. Days 31–35: S2.

## Día 1 — «El ruido» (S1)
**Beats:** El Narrador presents Camila Rey: astronomer, Bogotá, works at night at a small observatory in the hills. Her routine: coffee, computer screens, silence. At 3:47 in the morning, the computers record a noise that should not exist. She checks. It's gone. Mundo Real: what a radio telescope is and what it "hears" (it hears nothing — it sees invisible light). Repaso circles the core nouns.
**New:** la antena · la señal · el ruido · la estrella · escuchar · grabar · los datos · de repente · raro/rara · el observatorio

## Día 2 — «La rutina» (S1)
**Beats:** Camila's day in reverse: sleeps mornings, afternoon empanada at doña Marta's stall, night shift. She replays the recording; the noise has a shape. Her colleague Julio (aux_m) says: it's interference, always is. Mundo Real: why astronomers work at night — and why radio astronomers don't have to (but Camila's telescope shares time). Repaso.
**New:** el turno · dormir(se) · la pantalla · la grabación · la forma · la interferencia · el/la colega · revisar · casi · todavía

## Día 3 — «El taxi» (S1)
**Beats:** Meet El Flaco: Camila's regular taxi from Chapinero to the hills. He talks nonstop: his podcast «La Verdad Oculta», lights over Monserrate, the government hiding things. Camila half-listens, smiles. He says: "Doctora, el cielo está raro últimamente." She stops smiling. Mundo Real: Bogotá — altitude 2,640 m, the weather, why it's cold on the equator. Repaso.
**New:** el/la taxista · manejar · el programa · esconder · el gobierno · la luz / las luces · creer · quizás · últimamente · el cerro

## Día 4 — «Otra vez» (S1)
**Beats:** Night shift. 3:47 AM. The noise returns — same hour, same shape. Camila runs to the main screen, records everything. Julio, half asleep, sees her face and stops joking. Two occurrences is not chance, she says. Mundo Real: coincidence vs pattern — how scientists think (simply told). Repaso heavy on day-1 vocab (offset 3).
**New:** ocurrir · la casualidad · el patrón · exacto/exacta · despertar(se) · asustado/asustada · comprobar · dos veces · la hora · mismo/misma

## Día 5 — «El jefe» (S1)

## Día 6 — «La tormenta» (S1)
**Beats:** A huge storm over the hills. Lightning. The observatory loses power; backup generator coughs; and the main dish — la antena grande — is damaged. Camila watches the storm from the window, furious at the sky. If the signal comes at 3:47, they'll miss it: the dish is dead. Mundo Real: storms in the Andes; why equipment on mountains suffers. Repaso.
**New:** la tormenta · el rayo · la electricidad · el generador · dañar / dañado · la ventana · perder · funcionar · la lluvia (refresh) · el técnico

## Día 7 — «El venezolano» (S1)
**Beats:** The repair company sends an engineer: Andrés Materán, Venezuelan, toolbox and a thermos of coffee that he defends with his life. First Camila–Andrés scene: she's impatient, he's unhurried and precise. He fixes the generator in an hour and looks at the dish: "Esto está mal instalado desde el principio, doctora." First arepa mention (his lunch; her disdain; his horror at her disdain). Mundo Real: the Venezuelan migration — millions of neighbors, told with warmth, zero politics-lecture. Repaso. (Last day any English framing is allowed; from day 8 none.)
