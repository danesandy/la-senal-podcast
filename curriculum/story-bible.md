# La Señal — Story Bible

One year. One story. A signal that is not from Earth, and the people who fight
over what it means. Sci-fi mystery × political power struggle, set in Colombia
and Venezuela, told at exactly the Spanish level the listener can almost — but
not quite — keep up with.

## Premise

Dra. **Camila Rey**, a radio astronomer at a struggling observatory outside
Bogotá, detects a repeating anomaly nobody else believes is real. The only
person who takes her seriously is **Andrés Materán**, a Venezuelan electrical
engineer in Medellín — once a rising star at CANTV in Caracas, now fixing
antennas and driving deliveries like thousands of migrant professionals. As
evidence mounts that the signal is genuine, the question stops being scientific
and becomes political: who controls a discovery that changes everything?

## Tone

- Slow-burn mystery with monthly cliffhangers (Three Body Problem energy).
- Political scheming that rewards attention (House of Cards energy) — morally
  grey characters, shifting alliances, dialogue as chess.
- Warm, character-driven humor — never textbook-cute. The comedy lives in
  El Flaco's conspiracy theories (which keep being *accidentally* almost right),
  Tía Chela's voice notes, and the eternal Colombian–Venezuelan arepa rivalry
  between Camila and Andrés.

## Mundo Real segment (one per episode, tied to the chapter)

True documentary material worth knowing in any language, always thematically
hooked to the day's chapter. Rotating wells: radio astronomy & SETI history;
Colombian & Venezuelan geography/history (Bogotá, Medellín, the llanos,
Maracaibo, el Catatumbo lightning, Gran Colombia, the oil age, the migration);
food (the great arepa debate, ajiaco, pabellón); music (cumbia, vallenato,
gaita zuliana); science (prime numbers, radio waves, the Drake equation);
language itself (why paisas say "pues", Venezuelan "chévere", voseo pockets).

## Language & dialect policy

- Narrator scaffolds in clear Colombian Spanish; circling and repetition are
  his job, in-world and natural ("¿Y qué es una antena? Una antena es…").
- Camila/Bogotá characters use rolo markers; Andrés/Chela use Venezuelan
  markers (chévere, chamo, vaina — introduced in context, logged as slang).
- Regional slang is *content*, not decoration: each new slang item gets
  circled like vocabulary.
- No English after week 1 (≤10s framing max in days 1–7; none after day 7).

## Humor engines (recurring, low-vocab-cost)

1. El Flaco's theories: absurd premise, deadpan delivery, accidentally useful.
2. Arepa wars: Camila vs Andrés, escalating, resolved at the finale.
3. Tía Chela's voice notes: starts mundane, ends with a bombshell, every time.
4. Pardo's proverbs: mangles a saying every appearance; nobody corrects him.
